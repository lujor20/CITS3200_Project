Frequently Asked Questions:

JOKITOT is a cheating detection tool. JOKITOT is a collaborative project between Dr Guy Curtis, who is an Associate Professor in Applied Psychology at The University of Western Australia and six awfully talented students. Further contact details can be found in our Contacts page.

The findings presented do not serve as a concrete evidence of a cheating scandal. However, they can be used as indicators to support suspicion and aids further investigation. While a high suspicion score signifies a high chance of cheating, there are unconventional ways to trigger a high suspicion score even from a genuine document.

JOKITOT is highly influenced by Clare Johnson's paper "Understanding academic integrity and plagiarism in the digital age: Can digital forensics techniques help prevent and detect academic misconduct?" Clare Johnson's findings surrounding the OOXML document and its constituent XML parts inspired the team to develop a tool to detect suspicious flags based on document's analysis and log file.
